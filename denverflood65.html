Denver's Great Flood of June 1965

At 2 o'clock on the afternoon of June 16, 1965, incredibly intense rains fell south of Denver — 14 inches in 3 hours in some locations. By 8 pm, the South Platte River, by way of the Plum Creek basin, flooded Denver. It was the largest flooding event in Denver in over a century. By morning the flood had passed, but many areas adjacent to the Platte remained innundated by water, and homes, business, and infrastructure sustained damage. 28 people died and property damage came to $543,000,000. In order to mitigate against the future flooding of Denver, the U.S. Army Corps of Engineers began contruction on Chatfield Reservoir in 1967, finishing the project in 1973.
