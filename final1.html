Denver's Great Flood of June 1965

At 2 o'clock on the afternoon of June 16, 1965, incredibly intense rains fell south of Denver — 14 inches in 3 hours in some locations! — that resulted in flooding by 8 pm on the South Platte River by way of the Plum Creek basin. It was the largest flooding event in Denver in over a century. By morning the flood had passed, though many areas were innundated by water, and homes, business, and infrastructure sustained damage. 28 people died and property damage came to $543,000,000. In order to mitigate against the future flooding of Denver, the U.S. Army Corps of Engineers began contruction on Chatfield Reservoir in 1967 and finished in 1973.
